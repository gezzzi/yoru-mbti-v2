# 10 Fun Couple Quizzes to Take Together at Night

## 2. Love Language Quiz

**Best for:** Understanding how you and your partner express and receive love

Based on Gary Chapman's classic framework, the Love Language quiz identifies your primary love language: Words of Affirmation, Acts of Service, Receiving Gifts, Quality Time, or Physical Touch.

**Why it's great for couples:** Knowing your partner's love language helps you show affection in ways that actually resonate with them.

## 3. Attachment Style Quiz

**Best for:** Understanding your relationship anxiety and avoidance patterns

Attachment theory identifies four styles: Secure, Anxious, Avoidant, and Fearful-Avoidant. Understanding your attachment styles can explain why certain relationship patterns keep repeating.

**Why it matters:** If one partner is Anxious and the other is Avoidant, it creates a push-pull dynamic that's exhausting for both. Awareness is the first step to breaking the cycle.

## 4. The "36 Questions" Intimacy Exercise

**Best for:** Building emotional closeness quickly

Based on psychologist Arthur Aron's research, these 36 questions are designed to accelerate emotional intimacy. They start light ("Would you like to be famous?") and gradually deepen ("When did you last cry in front of another person?").

**Pro tip:** Do this one face-to-face with phones away. The eye contact at the end is what makes it powerful.

## 5. Couple Communication Style Quiz

**Best for:** Identifying how you handle conflict and conversation

Do you shut down during arguments? Get louder? Need time to process? Understanding your communication patterns helps prevent fights from escalating.

**Why it works:** Most relationship problems aren't about the actual issue - they're about **how** you talk about the issue.

## 6. Fantasy & Boundaries Quiz

**Best for:** Exploring desires safely

These quizzes let each partner privately indicate what they're curious about. Only mutual interests are revealed, so there's no risk of judgment.

**Safety note:** Always use tests that only show **overlapping** interests. Your private answers should stay private.

## 7. Relationship Values Assessment

**Best for:** Long-term compatibility check

This type of quiz measures alignment on big-picture topics: kids, finances, career priorities, family involvement, and lifestyle goals.

**Best timing:** Great for couples who are getting serious and want to make sure they're aligned on the fundamentals.

## 8. Emotional Intelligence Quiz (Taken Together)

**Best for:** Understanding each other's emotional awareness

Take an EQ test individually, then compare scores. Areas where you differ often explain recurring misunderstandings.

**Insight:** High EQ doesn't mean being emotional - it means being **aware** of emotions (yours and your partner's).

## 9. Stress Response Quiz

**Best for:** Understanding how each other handles pressure

Some people want to talk through stress, others need alone time. Some exercise, others withdraw. Knowing your partner's stress response prevents you from taking their behavior personally.

**The takeaway:** "They're not ignoring me - they're processing."

## 10. Future Vision Board Quiz

**Best for:** Dreaming together

This isn't a traditional quiz, but a guided exercise where each partner describes their ideal life 5 years from now. Compare your visions and find the overlaps.

**Why it's powerful:** Shared goals create shared motivation. And discovering differences early gives you time to align.

## How to Make Quiz Night a Regular Thing

### Set the Scene
- Put phones on "Do Not Disturb"
- Get comfortable - couch, bed, or blankets on the floor
- Have drinks and snacks ready
- No screens except for the quiz itself

### Ground Rules
1. **No judgment.** Every answer is valid.
2. **Listen more than react.** Let your partner finish before responding.
3. **It's about understanding, not winning.** There are no right or wrong answers.
4. **What happens in quiz night stays in quiz night.** Don't use answers against each other later.

### After the Quiz
- Discuss 2-3 things that surprised you
- Share one thing you appreciate about your partner
- Pick one insight to work on together this week
